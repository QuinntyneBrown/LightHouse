# L2 — Detailed Requirements: LightHouse Kids UI Design

---

## L1-1: Child-Facing Home Screen

### L2-1.1: Age-Band Welcome Banner
The home screen shall display a welcoming hero section with the child's name/avatar and a warm greeting. The visual style adapts to the active age band (soft pastels for Seedlings, bright primary colors for Sprouts, adventurous tones for Explorers).
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Hero section is visible at the top of the home screen, displays child name and avatar, and uses age-band-appropriate color palette.

### L2-1.2: Featured Content Carousel
The home screen shall include a horizontally scrollable featured content carousel showcasing 4–6 editorially curated items with large thumbnail cards, titles, and content type icons.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: A horizontal carousel of content cards is present below the hero, each card shows a thumbnail image area, title, and content type indicator.

### L2-1.3: Category Quick-Access Row
The home screen shall display a row of large, icon-driven category buttons (Bible Stories, Songs, Prayers, Devotionals, Activities) for quick navigation.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: A row of at least 5 category buttons with icons and labels is visible, each button is large enough for child interaction (minimum 64px touch target).

### L2-1.4: Curated Playlist Section
The home screen shall show 2–3 curated playlist cards (e.g., "Bedtime Stories," "Sunday Morning Songs") with cover art and item count.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Playlist cards are displayed in a horizontal scroll or grid, each showing cover art, playlist title, and number of items.

## L1-2: Content Playback Experience

### L2-2.1: Video Player Screen
The playback screen shall show a full-width video player with simple, oversized play/pause, rewind (10s), and forward (10s) controls. A content title and series name appear below the player.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Video player area occupies full width, controls are large and clearly visible, title and series info displayed below.

### L2-2.2: Playlist Queue
Below the player, a vertical list of upcoming items in the current playlist shall be displayed with thumbnails, titles, and durations.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: A scrollable list of playlist items appears below the player with thumbnail, title, and duration for each item.

### L2-2.3: Audio Player Mode
For audio content, the screen shall display album/cover art prominently with playback controls, a progress bar, and the content title.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Audio player shows large cover art, progress bar, and playback controls centered on screen.

## L1-3: Onboarding & Profile Management

### L2-3.1: Welcome & Sign-Up Screen
The onboarding shall begin with a branded welcome screen showing the LightHouse Kids logo, tagline, and options to sign up via email or OAuth (Google, Apple).
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Welcome screen displays logo, tagline ("A safe place to explore God's Word"), and sign-up buttons for email, Google, and Apple.

### L2-3.2: Child Profile Creation
After sign-up, the parent shall be guided to create a child profile by entering a name/nickname, selecting an age band, and choosing an avatar from a set of friendly character options.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Profile creation screen has name input, age-band selector (Seedlings/Sprouts/Explorers), and an avatar picker with at least 6 character options.

### L2-3.3: PIN Setup
The parent shall be prompted to set a 4-digit PIN that protects access to parent settings and profile management.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: PIN setup screen with a 4-digit numeric input and confirmation step is present.

### L2-3.4: Profile Switcher
A profile switcher shall be accessible from the app shell, showing all child profiles under the account with their avatars, allowing quick switching.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Profile switcher is accessible from the main navigation, displays all child profile avatars and names, and allows selection.

## L1-4: Parental Controls Dashboard

### L2-4.1: Parent Dashboard Overview
The parent dashboard shall show a summary of each child's recent activity including content watched, time spent, and current screen time limit status.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Dashboard displays per-child cards with watch time summary, recent content titles, and screen time limit indicator.

### L2-4.2: Screen Time Settings
The dashboard shall include a screen time configuration panel where parents can set daily time limits per child profile with a visual slider or input.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Screen time panel shows a configurable daily limit control per child with current usage displayed.

### L2-4.3: Content Preview & Blocking
The dashboard shall allow parents to browse the content library in a list/grid view with options to preview and block/hide specific items.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Content list with preview button and block/hide toggle is present in the parent dashboard.

### L2-4.4: Viewing History
The dashboard shall show a chronological viewing history per child with content title, date, and duration watched.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: A scrollable viewing history list per child is displayed with title, date, and watch duration.

## L1-5: Content Browsing & Playlists

### L2-5.1: Category Browse Screen
Each content category shall have a dedicated browse screen showing a filterable grid of content cards with thumbnails, titles, age badges, and duration.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Category screen displays content in a grid layout with thumbnail, title, age band badge, and duration on each card.

### L2-5.2: Playlist Detail Screen
Each playlist shall have a detail screen showing the playlist cover, title, description, and an ordered list of content items.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Playlist screen shows cover art, title, description, item count, and an ordered list of items with play buttons.

### L2-5.3: Search Screen
A search screen shall allow parents/children to search by title or category with results displayed as content cards.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Search screen has a prominent search input and displays results as content cards matching the query.

---

## L1-6: Navigation & App Shell

### L2-6.1: Bottom Tab Bar
The app shall use a bottom tab bar with 4 tabs: Home, Browse, Playlists, and Parent (lock icon). Icons are large and labeled.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Bottom tab bar is present with 4 labeled icon tabs, each at least 48px touch target height.

### L2-6.2: App Theme & Branding
The app shall use the LightHouse Kids brand identity with a lighthouse motif, warm color palette (ocean blues, sunset golds, soft whites), rounded corners, and friendly typography.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: The design uses a consistent warm color palette, rounded UI elements, lighthouse branding, and a child-friendly sans-serif font.

### L2-6.3: Screen Time Notification Overlay
When a child's screen time limit is reached, a friendly full-screen overlay shall appear with a message like "Great job today! Time to take a break!" with a lighthouse illustration.
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Full-screen overlay with friendly message, illustration, and no dismiss option for children (only parent PIN can extend) is designed.
